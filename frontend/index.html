Zhong Kui

Introduction

Zhong Kui is an important figure in traditional Chinese culture, often depicted as a deity who captures ghosts and exorcises evil spirits. His image is typically fierce-looking, holding a sword, symbolizing justice and strength.

Historical Background

The legend of Zhong Kui originated in the Tang Dynasty. It is said that he was a brilliant scholar who failed the imperial examination due to his ugly appearance and eventually committed suicide. After his death, he was appointed by the Jade Emperor as the God of Ghost Hunting.

Cultural Significance

Zhong Kui holds an important place in Chinese folk beliefs. People often hang his portraits in their homes to ward off evil spirits. His image is also widely featured in operas, paintings, and literary works.
